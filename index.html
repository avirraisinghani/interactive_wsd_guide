Hey there, Street Smart Champ!

Street dogs mostly want to rest and eat in peace. But how you act around them matters a lot. Read each situation, learn the right move, and earn points as you go.

Watch Out
🍪
Teasing a Dog with Food
The biscuit wave trap
▼
What happensYou hold out a biscuit, the dog walks toward you, then you snatch it back or run away. The dog feels tricked and unsafe. A dog that feels unsafe may growl or snap.
🚨The dog bites your hand. You go to hospital. Nobody wins.
💡Place food on the ground and step back. Let the dog come to it on its own.
😤
Running Toward or Chasing a Dog
The chase game gone wrong
▼
What happensYou run toward a sleeping dog screaming to startle it for fun. The dog wakes up scared and confused. A scared dog either runs or bites.
🚨The dog bites one of your friends on the leg before running away.
💡If you see a sleeping dog, walk around it quietly. Never shout at or run toward a resting dog.
🥩
Going Near a Dog That is Eating
The mealtime mistake
▼
What happensA street dog is eating near a bin. You walk up and try to pet it. Even calm dogs snap when they feel their food is being taken.
🚨The dog growls and bites your hand.
💡Never go near a dog that is eating, chewing, or drinking. Wait until it walks away on its own.
Be Careful
👀
Staring Into a Dog's Eyes
The eye contact challenge
▼
What happensYou stare straight into a street dog's eyes for a long time. In dog language, a long hard stare is a challenge or threat.
⚠The dog reads this as you picking a fight. It may bark, chase, or bite.
💡Look away slowly and turn your body slightly to the side.
🌙
Walking Alone Past Dogs at Night
The late night walk
▼
What happensAt night, street dogs are more active and protective of their area. A group may bark and follow you if you walk through a lane alone.
⚠You panic and run. Running always makes it worse.
💡Walk slowly and steadily. Stand still for a few seconds if they approach, then move away calmly.
Smart Moves
🤲
How to Greet a Street Dog Properly
The right way to say hello
▼
The right approachCrouch to one side, not directly facing the dog. Extend your hand low, palm down. Let the dog sniff you first. If it wags and comes closer, pet under its chin or chest. Never reach over its head straight away.
✅The dog sniffs you, wags, and accepts your touch. A calm happy moment for both.
+20 points earned
🧍
If a Dog Charges at You
The freeze rule
▼
What to doStop moving immediately. Stand still like a tree. Cross your arms. Look away. Most dogs will slow down and walk off when you stop reacting.
✅You stand still. The dog barks once, then walks away. You are safe.
+20 points earned
Golden Rules
🤫
Never scream near a dog
🏃
Never run from a barking dog
🙅
Never throw things at dogs
👶
Tell an adult if a dog seems hurt
🐾
Give dogs space on the pavement
💧

A Word Before You Step Out

For older adults, a bite can mean weeks of treatment and real stress. Nearly all street dog bites are avoidable. Learn what triggers them and how to protect yourself every day.

High Risk Situations
🦯
Raising a Stick or Umbrella Near a Dog
When a walking aid becomes a threat
▼
What happensOut of fear, you raise your walking stick or umbrella to shoo a dog away. The dog does not know you are scared. It sees something raised at it, which reads as an attack.
🚨The dog lunges and bites your arm or hand before you can react.
💡Keep your stick pointed down near a dog. If you need space, move it slowly and low to the side. Never raise it above your waist.
🌅
Unfamiliar Routes on Morning Walks
The 5 AM danger zone
▼
What happensEarly morning, street dogs are most active and most protective of their area. Walking through a lane you do not usually use can set off a territorial response.
🚨A group of dogs follow you barking. You walk faster. Walking faster makes them more aggressive.
💡Stick to familiar routes. Dogs there already know you. If dogs follow you, slow your pace, face slightly away, and walk steadily.
⚔
Stepping Between Two Fighting Dogs
The peacemaker mistake
▼
What happensTwo dogs are fighting. Out of kindness, you step between them. In the heat of a fight, a dog does not see you as a helper. It sees a third threat and redirects its bite.
🚨You receive a serious bite. This is one of the most common ways senior citizens get bitten.
💡Never step between fighting dogs. Walk away. Dog fights almost always end on their own within seconds.
Everyday Cautions
🛍
Carrying Fish or Meat from the Market
The market walk
▼
What happensYou walk home with fish or raw meat in an open bag. Dogs can smell this from far away and may follow you or try to grab the bag.
⚠A dog jumps for the bag and scratches or bites you.
💡Always seal raw meat inside a closed bag. If a dog approaches, lower the bag to your side and keep walking without reacting.
🐶
Walking Past a Mother Dog with Puppies
The mother's instinct
▼
What happensA mother dog is resting with her puppies near a doorstep. You walk past or bend down to look. A mother protecting her young is the most protective a dog will ever be.
⚠The mother bites without much warning.
💡Cross to the other side of the road. Do not crouch near the puppies. Give the family a wide, respectful gap.
Smart Habits
🚶
Passing a Dog Safely on the Pavement
The daily walk formula
▼
The safe methodGive the dog at least 2 to 3 metres of space by stepping wide around it. Walk at a normal steady pace. Do not stop suddenly, stare, or talk loudly. A dog not given a reason to react will almost never react.
✅You walk past calmly. The dog looks up briefly, then goes back to resting.
+20 points earned
🆘
If You Are Bitten, Here is What to Do
Quick response steps
▼
Step by stepWash the wound with soap and water for at least 10 minutes. Do not cover it tightly right away. See a doctor the same day. Do not wait. Most WSD-program dogs are vaccinated, but always follow your doctor's advice.
✅You wash the wound, see a doctor that day, and complete the treatment. You recover fully.
+20 points earned
